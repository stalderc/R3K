A Great Resume

We are developing the Resumator3000 to assist students in developing their first resume for the professional market that awaits them upon completion of a degree. Our product allows the student to focus on writing facts about themselves and not have to worry about format, layout or section order to present information. R3k will take an inventory of the student over time and present a finished professionally designed resume on demand. The added benefit of the service is that students will have one place that is free of “social network” distractions and focuses solely on getting the most important document that we write as students into a form that will present us in the best light for employers.

Any secondary purposes?

Certainly. A link to the student’s resume will be available and will only display the resume of that student. This will act as a students official digital resume that is constantly up-to-date and properly formatted. No additional information (ads, links to other students, distractions in general) will be presented on this page. Just a simple clean presentation of the students resume. So, for example, if a student is in an interview and is asked for a copy of their resume a quick send of a link will have the interviewer at the students resume. No need to find a printer or send a pdf and then print; let’s remove all of those hassles.

Technology Used

Our team will be using a few different languages to implement our project. The database will be Oracle and we'll write the bulk of our code using PL/SQL which includes a ton of SQL queries. Some folks may not have heard of PL/SQL; you can think of it as PHP for Oracle. Last semester in our IS420 (Database Applications) class we learned this language and produced the first prototype of R3k. We're not recycling too much of that code for this project but we will be using the knowledge gained during that class to build the Resumator3000. It's anticipated that we'll be working with this language for the bulk of our coding and the results will be on full display when we launch the beta.

Web Oriented Technologies

For other technology minded people who may read this page; it should be obvious that we're implementing with HTML5, CSS3 and JavaScript as well. For the backend services we're using Oracle's PL/SQL in conjunction with PSP pages. These core technologies are essential in accomplishing our goals of a working system by the conclusion of the Spring 2013 semester. Many of the Teamators are also taking a web scripting class this semester which should help bolster our overall group knowledge of the technology and allow for relatively quick development of the pages we'll need in the upcoming weeks.
